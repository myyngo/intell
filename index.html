Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968
Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1971
First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing devices.

1978
8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

1985
386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

2006
Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. Over subsequent years, Intel invests heavily in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse this trend.

2020
RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals, aiming to drive industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

2022
Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announces its commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040, building on years of environmental initiatives.

2023
Renewable Electricity

The company achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, helping to drastically lower carbon emissions and driving progress toward Intel's long-term sustainability goals.

2024
Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

RISE Strategy

Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling

Intel's RISE strategy represents our holistic approach to corporate responsibility and sustainability. Launched in 2020, this framework guides our actions across four key pillars:

  • Responsible: Conducting business with integrity and transparency
  • Inclusive: Creating opportunities for all people and communities
  • Sustainable: Minimizing environmental impact and driving climate action
  • Enabling: Leveraging technology to solve global challenges

By 2030, Intel aims to achieve carbon neutrality in our operations, use 100% renewable electricity, and restore more water than we consume. We're also committed to zero waste to landfill and advancing circular economy practices across our supply chain.

2030 Goal: Drive industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Net-Zero Commitment

Achieving Net-Zero by 2040

In 2022, Intel made a groundbreaking commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across our global operations by 2040. This ambitious goal covers Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions and represents one of the most aggressive climate commitments in the semiconductor industry.

Our Pathway to Net-Zero:

Energy Efficiency

Investing in energy-efficient manufacturing and facility operations

Renewable Energy

Transitioning to 100% renewable electricity globally

Carbon Removal

Investing in carbon capture and natural climate solutions

Industry Leadership

Collaborating with suppliers and partners to reduce emissions

Progress Update: Intel achieved 99% renewable electricity usage in 2023, well ahead of schedule.

Water & Waste Initiatives

Conserving Water, Reducing Waste

Water is essential to semiconductor manufacturing, and Intel takes its responsibility seriously. We've implemented comprehensive water conservation and restoration programs while pioneering circular economy practices to minimize waste.

Water Stewardship

Intel has conserved billions of gallons of water through innovative technologies and process improvements. Our goal is to restore 100% of the water we consume for global operations by 2030, with a focus on supporting watersheds in water-stressed regions.

Circular Economy Leadership

Intel recycles or upcycles over 90% of our manufacturing waste. Materials that can't be reused are transformed into new products, keeping valuable resources in use and out of landfills.
Multiple Intel facilities have achieved zero waste to landfill certification, diverting 100% of waste through recycling, composting, and waste-to-energy programs.
We work closely with suppliers to reduce packaging waste, optimize material use, and implement circular economy principles throughout our entire value chain.
Impact: Intel restored over 9 billion gallons of water in 2023 through conservation and watershed restoration projects.
